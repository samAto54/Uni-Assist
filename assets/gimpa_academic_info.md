The Ghana Institute of Management and Public Administration (GIMPA) stands as a beacon of academic excellence in West Africa, offering a diverse array of courses designed to meet the evolving needs of students and professionals alike. Established in 1961, GIMPA has transformed from a specialized training institution for civil servants into a comprehensive academic powerhouse with programs spanning business, public administration, law, technology, and the liberal arts.

What makes GIMPA’s academic offerings particularly valuable is their thoughtful design, balancing theoretical foundations with practical applications to address real-world challenges. Each program maintains rigorous standards while remaining accessible to qualified candidates through clearly defined admission pathways. The institution’s strong connections to both public and private sectors ensure that curricula remain relevant to current market demands and developmental priorities.

This comprehensive guide explores the full spectrum of GIMPA’s course offerings across certificate, diploma, undergraduate, and postgraduate levels. We examine the specific admission requirements for each program category, helping prospective students identify suitable educational pathways aligned with their qualifications and career aspirations. Whether you’re a high school graduate planning your tertiary education journey, a professional seeking specialized training, or someone considering an advanced degree, understanding GIMPA’s diverse academic landscape is the first step toward making an informed educational choice.

GIMPA’s Academic Structure
Six Academic Schools
GIMPA’s academic programs are organized into six distinct schools, each specializing in different but interconnected disciplines:

GIMPA Business School: Focuses on business administration, finance, marketing, and related disciplines.
School of Public Service and Governance: Specializes in public administration, policy analysis, and governance studies.
Faculty of Law: Offers programs in legal education from undergraduate through postgraduate levels.
School of Technology: Provides education in information technology, computing, and digital innovations.
School of Liberal Arts and Social Sciences: Covers humanities, social sciences, and interdisciplinary studies.
School of Research and Graduate Studies: Oversees research activities and advanced postgraduate programs.
This organizational structure allows GIMPA to maintain specialized focus in distinct academic domains while facilitating interdisciplinary collaboration where beneficial.

Program Levels Offered
GIMPA delivers education across multiple qualification levels:

Certificate Programs: Short-term, focused courses typically lasting several weeks to a few months.
Diploma Programs: Intermediate qualifications usually completed in 1-2 years.
Undergraduate Degrees: Full bachelor’s degree programs typically requiring 3-4 years of study.
Postgraduate Diplomas: Advanced qualifications for degree holders, often completed in one year.
Master’s Degrees: Advanced degree programs typically requiring 1-2 years beyond the bachelor’s level.
Doctoral Programs: The highest academic qualification, typically requiring 3+ years of original research.
This multi-level approach provides educational pathways for diverse student populations, from those entering tertiary education to professionals seeking specialized or advanced training.

Frequently Asked Questions About GIMPA Courses
Does GIMPA offer distance learning or online programs?
While GIMPA has been developing blended learning approaches, information on fully online programs is limited in the provided materials. The institution has traditionally emphasized in-person education, though technological integration is part of its strategic vision. Prospective students interested in distance learning should check the current offerings on the GIMPA website or contact the admissions office directly.

Can I transfer credits from another institution to GIMPA?
GIMPA may accept transfer credits from accredited institutions on a case-by-case basis. The process typically involves evaluation of course content, credit hours, and grades achieved. Transfer applicants should provide detailed transcripts and course descriptions from their previous institution. The maximum transferable credits are usually limited to ensure sufficient GIMPA residency.

What language proficiency is required for GIMPA programs?
English is the official language of instruction for all GIMPA programs. While specific language proficiency tests (like TOEFL or IELTS) aren’t explicitly mentioned in the requirements, proficiency in written and spoken English is essential for successful participation. International students from non-English speaking countries may need to demonstrate language competency.

Are there age restrictions for GIMPA programs?
There are no strict upper age limits for GIMPA programs. For undergraduate admission through the standard pathway, applicants typically enter after completing secondary education. The mature student route provides an alternative for those at least 25 years old. Postgraduate programs often benefit from age diversity, with many participants bringing substantial work experience.

What is the difference between regular and executive programs?
Regular programs follow standard academic scheduling with weekday classes and are typically designed for full-time or part-time study without assuming extensive prior experience. Executive programs are structured for working professionals with significant experience (often 5+ years), featuring weekend or modular scheduling, cohort-based learning, and curricula that leverage participants’ professional backgrounds. Executive programs usually carry higher fees but offer specialized networking and content tailored to experienced professionals.

## GIMPA Overview & Identity

GIMPA was established in 1961 as a joint Ghana Government and United Nations special fund project, originally named the Institute of Public Administration. It was set up to develop Ghana's public administrative system and train civil servants. In 1966 the UN handed it over to the Government of Ghana. In 1969 it was renamed the Ghana Institute of Management and Public Administration (GIMPA) to reflect its expanded functions. It is governed by the GIMPA Act, 2004 (Act 676) and is accredited by the National Accreditation Board. GIMPA is an independent public tertiary institution with financial and operational autonomy — it operates without direct government funding. As of 2026, GIMPA is celebrating its 65th anniversary (GIMPA@65). The September 2026 intake admission is currently in progress.

GIMPA's mission is to maintain a Centre of Excellence for training in public and business administration, continuously enhancing the capability of middle and top-level executives in public and private sectors as well as NGOs, both in Ghana and internationally. GIMPA has over 6,000 students enrolled across four campuses.

## GIMPA Counselling Unit

The GIMPA Counselling Unit (GCU) provides confidential and professional mental health services to students experiencing emotional distress. Services include: initial evaluations, individual counselling, group counselling, career counselling, consultations on mental health issues (for staff, faculty, and family members of students), psychoeducational workshops, and external referrals for higher levels of care.

To make an appointment: book online at http://scheduler.gimpa.edu.gh/ea/ or email gcu@gimpa.edu.gh. Both in-person and virtual appointments are available.

The Wellness Ambassador Programme: GIMPA students in good standing can volunteer as Wellness Ambassadors to support the Counselling Centre with outreaches and new student orientations. Email gcu@gimpa.edu.gh to join.
